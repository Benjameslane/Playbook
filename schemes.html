In-depth Schemes: Air Raid

Oregon Spread


The Oregon Spread offense, pioneered by Chip Kelly, is known for its high tempo and explosive plays. This scheme utilizes a no-huddle approach to keep defenses on their heels, with a heavy emphasis on quick, short passes and a strong running game. Learn how to implement the Oregon Spread to maximize your offensive efficiency and keep your opponents guessing.


Mike Leach Air Raid


The Air Raid, popularized by Mike Leach, focuses on a pass-heavy attack designed to spread out the defense. This scheme relies on precision passing and versatile receiver routes to create mismatches and open space.


Navy Triple Option


The Navy Triple Option offense is a timeless strategy centered around the option run game. This scheme emphasizes disciplined execution and decision-making, with the quarterback reading the defense and making quick decisions to hand off, keep, or pitch the ball. Use the Navy Triple Option to control the clock and wear down defenses.


Stanford Power Run


The Stanford Power Run, led by Jim Harbaugh during his tenure, is built on physicality and a strong running game. This scheme uses a combination of power runs and play-action passes to dominate the line of scrimmage and control the tempo. Incorporate the Stanford Power Run to impose your will on the defense and establish a dominant ground game.


Nevada Pistol


The Nevada Pistol, developed by Chris Ault, combines elements of the shotgun and single-back formations to create a versatile and balanced attack. This scheme allows for a diverse range of running and passing plays, making it difficult for defenses to predict. Implement the Nevada Pistol to keep your offense dynamic and adaptable.
