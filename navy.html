Navy Triple Option Offense

Overview

The Navy Triple Option offense is a timeless strategy centered around the option run game. This scheme emphasizes disciplined execution and decision-making, with the quarterback reading the defense and making quick decisions to hand off, keep, or pitch the ball. Use the Navy Triple Option to control the clock and wear down defenses.

Success and Impact

The Triple Option has been a cornerstone of Navy's football success for decades. Its unique approach and disciplined execution have allowed Navy to compete effectively against more athletically gifted teams. The scheme's ability to control the game tempo and minimize turnovers has been key to its sustained success.

Player Archetypes

  • Quarterback: Smart, tough, and quick decision-makers who can execute the option reads flawlessly and handle the physical demands of running the ball.
  • Running Backs: Durable and agile backs who excel in vision and can make quick cuts, and are comfortable with handling the ball multiple times per game.
  • Wide Receivers: Strong blockers who can also be threats in the passing game when defenses overcommit to stopping the run.
  • Offensive Line: Smaller, quicker linemen who can execute cut blocks and sustain blocks in space effectively.

Key Plays

  • Inside Veer: A classic triple option play where the quarterback reads the defensive end to decide whether to hand off to the fullback, keep the ball, or pitch to the trailing back.
  • Midline Option: Similar to the Inside Veer but with a read on the defensive tackle instead, creating additional inside running lanes.
  • Outside Veer: An option play that targets the perimeter, stretching the defense horizontally and creating opportunities for big gains.
  • Counter Option: A misdirection play that starts like the Inside Veer but reverses direction to catch the defense off guard.

Implementation Tips

Implementing the Navy Triple Option requires rigorous practice and a strong commitment to discipline. Focus on perfecting the option reads and ensuring all players understand their assignments. Emphasize ball security and consistent execution to minimize turnovers and maximize time of possession.
